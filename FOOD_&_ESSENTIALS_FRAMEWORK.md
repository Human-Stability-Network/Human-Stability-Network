# Food & Essentials Program Framework

## Mission

Ensure no participant goes hungry or lacks basic necessities while building stability.

## Program Components

### 1. Emergency Food (Immediate)
- Same-day grocery delivery
- Meal vouchers (restaurants/food trucks)
- School meal coordination for children

### 2. Weekly Essentials (Ongoing)
- Grocery allowance ($50/person/week)
- Fresh produce access
- Cultural/religious dietary accommodation

### 3. Non-Food Essentials
- Hygiene products (soap, toothpaste, diapers)
- Cleaning supplies
- Clothing vouchers ($200/season/child)

### 4. Special Circumstances
- Medical diet support
- Infant formula/baby food
- Menstrual products (unlimited)

## Delivery Models

| Model | Best For | Implementation |
|-------|----------|----------------|
| Direct allowance | Stable housing | Prepaid grocery card |
| Delivery | Mobility challenges | Partner with DoorDash/Instacart |
| Pickup points | Community hubs | HSN food lockers |
| Meal programs | Children/seniors | School & senior center meals |

## Eligibility

- All enrolled participants automatically eligible
- No means testing within HSN
- Children prioritized for enhanced benefits

## AI Support Integration

- **Meal planning** - AI generates budget-friendly recipes
- **Allergy management** - Tracks dietary restrictions
- **Waste reduction** - Suggests usage before expiration
- **Benefit optimization** - Coordinates with SNAP/WIC

## Success Metrics

- Food security rate (USDA scale): >95%
- Diet quality score: >80% fresh/whole foods
- Meal skipping rate: <2% of days

## Sustainability

- 70% of food budget from AI Value Engine
- 20% from food bank partnerships (in-kind)
- 10% from community donations
