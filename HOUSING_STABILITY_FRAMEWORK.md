# Housing Stability Program Framework

## Mission

Ensure every participant has safe, stable housing as the foundation for rebuilding their life.

## Program Components

### 1. Emergency Housing (0-30 days)
- Hotel/motel vouchers
- Shelter coordination
- Rapid rehousing placement

### 2. Bridge Housing (1-6 months)
- Subsidized short-term rentals
- Shared housing matching
- Utility assistance

### 3. Long-Term Stability (6-24 months)
- Rental subsidies (decreasing over time)
- Landlord mediation
- Lease coaching

### 4. Pathway to Ownership (24+ months)
- Down payment assistance
- Credit repair program
- Financial literacy (housing focus)

## Eligibility

- Enrolled HSN participant
- Housing insecurity or homelessness
- Willing to work with housing navigator

## Participant Commitment

| Phase | Participant Action | HSN Support |
|-------|-------------------|-------------|
| Emergency | Daily check-in | 100% subsidy |
| Bridge | Job/education activity | 80% subsidy |
| Long-term | Savings plan | 50%→25%→0% |
| Ownership | Savings accumulation | Matching funds |

## Housing Navigator Role

- 1:50 caseload maximum
- Weekly check-ins first 90 days
- Monthly thereafter
- Landlord relationship management

## Success Metrics

- Time to housing: <14 days (emergency), <60 days (permanent)
- Housing retention: >90% at 12 months
- Rent burden reduction: >30% of income to <30%

## Integration Points

- **Food Program** - Meal delivery during housing search
- **Phone Program** - Navigator communication
- **AI Support** - Landlord email drafting, application help
- **Trust Fund** - Rent assistance drawdowns
